Sainthood vs Sinfulness

Kanye West's ambitious and experimental seventh studio album, The Life of Pablo, is a genre-bending odyssey that defies easy categorization. Released in February 2016, after a recording process that stretched from 2013, the album went through numerous delays and alterations, even after its initial surprise drop. Unlike some of his previous concept albums, The Life of Pablo isn't a tightly woven narrative. Instead, it explores a tapestry of themes that are central to Kanye's life and worldview. Faith, both religious and in himself, brushes shoulders with the darker aspects of fame, materialism, and his own self-perception.

The title of the album reflects this duality as "Pablo" refeers to three people, Pablo Picasso (A famous spanish artist), Pablo Escobar (A colombian druglord, narcoterrorist and politician) and St Paul (one of the most important people in the history of Christianity).

These three figures - artist, criminal, and saint - embody Kanye's inner conflicts. He craves artistic merit while facing the allure of wealth and fame. The music mirrors this struggle, gospel blending with industrial beats, braggadocio with vulnerability. Unflinching and rewarding, The Life of Pablo is a testament to Kanye's audacious vision and his wrestle with his own complexities.
